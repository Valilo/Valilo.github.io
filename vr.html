Lost in the Retroverse: A VR Experience

"Lost in the Retroverse" is a Virtual Reality experience that takes you through the grief of losing a loved one and immerses you in the nostalgia of iconic video games like Tetris, Super Mario, and Pacman. Inspired by the most iconic games of the 80s, it presents the adventure of Alex, a teenager who experiences the loss of his father but soon discovers Retroverse: a gaming dimension that keeps his father's memory alive. The primary goal of this project was to design and create a visually stunning experience, that would immerse the user in an imaginary journey to a virtual world. Lost in the Retroverse is comprised of a variety of creative components: Narrative, 3D Modelling, Game Mechanics and User Interactions, Audio, Graphic Design and Illustrations.

It received favorable appraisals from both academics and exhibition attendees.

A cutting-edge VR Installation: "Visualising the Voice of Maria Callas"

After an intensive educational workshop held at the Stavros Niarchos Foundation Cultural Center (SNFCC) in Athens, this VR Exhibition aims to expand the connections between architecture and music, space and sound. Utilising new digital media, the exhibition experimentally visualises the iconic soprano Maria Callas's extraordinary voice. The workshop was a collaborative effort between the Greek National Opera and the DmLab at the Technical University of Crete. This initiative coincides with the 100th-anniversary celebration of mariacallas, a part of the 2023 UNESCO Maria Callas Anniversary proposed by the Hellenic Ministry of Culture and Sports
