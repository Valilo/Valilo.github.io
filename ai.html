Research Paper (grade: 85/100)

As part of the requirements for the degree of Master of Science Interactive Digital Media, Trinity College Dublin:
"Exploring the Impact of AI and Deep Learning in Digital Scenography"

Abstract:
This paper explores the applications of AI and deep learning systems in digital scenography, with a specific focus on their role in scenic design processes. Digital scenography refers to the use of digital tools in creating stage settings and visual elements for live performances, such as theatre and opera productions. This interdisciplinary field encompasses both performance practices and design processes. For the purposes of this paper, the scope is narrowed to the utilisation of AI tools in the scenic design process. Within the design process, the emphasis is placed on the pivotal stage of content generation, where the interplay between digital tools and digital scenography unfolds. In recent times, the emergence of AI-enhanced tools has revolutionised the generation of concept art, enabling designers to amplify their imagination and creativity and produce innovative ideas with unprecedented speed and quality. These cutting-edge tools, categorized as image-to-image and text-to-image systems, harness the latest advancements in AI and deep learning. By seamlessly blending artistic styles and arbitrary images or by interpreting simple textual prompts provided by designers, these systems generate appealing concept art that revitalises the artistic process. The paper's findings revolve around the efficiency of these AI accelerated systems in generating concept art. Not only do they provide contemporary artists with invigorating ideas, but they also expedite the overall workflow, allowing designers to actualise their vision with greater efficiency.


Computer Engineering Thesis (grade: 10/10)

As part of the requirements for the Integrated Master of Engineering in Computer Engineering and Informatics, University of Patras:
"Creation of Artificial Artworks using Specific Styles with Convolutional Neural Networks": Transforming arbitrary high resolution images to artistic versions similar to famous paintings using pre-trained networks on image classification

Abstract:
In this thesis, Neural Style Transfer methodologies are presented that create artificial artworks using as references famous paintings. Deep Learning techniques are utilized for Neural Style Transfer and in particular Convolutional Neural Networks pre-trained on image classification. The creation of the final image results from the artistic rendering of the initial image, based on a selected painting. For the artificial artwork to be produced, either an image-optimization-based neural method is used, transferring only one style each time, or a model-optimization-based neural method for the transfer of arbitrary styles. With the latter method, a variety of artificial artworks are created, using images of small size up to high resolution images. Moreover, instead of utilizing the entire artistic image, the use of random patches of the painting is proposed to create detailed high-resolution artificial images, without visual artifacts and with a representative depiction of the painting's style. Finally, experiments of successive artistic transformation of the initial image are performed using two different artistic styles. The final artificial images are remarkable results with high aesthetic quality and can be subject to further artistic adjustments, while at the same time they highlight the problem of content and style disentanglement.
